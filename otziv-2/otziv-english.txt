REVIEW
by the scientific supervisor on the bachelor's thesis
of the SPbU undergraduate Goroshko Andrei Vitalievich,
educational programme СВ.5001.2022 "Mathematics and Computer Science",
on the topic "Analysis of the impact of YandexGPT fine-tuning on the
correctness of function selection for retrieving data by the original
user query"

    The bachelor's thesis of A. V. Goroshko is devoted to an applied
problem that has gained notable importance in recent years in the
practice of building production AI agents based on large language
models — the problem of tool selection. The author formalises it as a
binary classification task on the first function call and conducts a
systematic experimental study of how parameter-efficient fine-tuning of
a model can raise classification quality to a production-grade level.

    From an engineering perspective the work is performed at a high
level. A. V. Goroshko independently designed and implemented in Python
a modular software framework that supports the full reproducible cycle:
generation and validation of training and evaluation datasets,
conversion to JSONL, fine-tuning via the Yandex Cloud API, evaluation,
parsing of tool calls and automatic computation of metrics. The
architecture relies on dependency injection and a plug-in model of
LLM clients, static type-checking with mypy and runtime data validation
with pydantic, which matches modern standards for research code.

    A substantively strong feature of the work is its rigorous
statistical protocol. The author correctly handles multiple
comparisons: Cochran's Q test is applied to the global hypothesis of
homogeneity across the models, the exact McNemar test with a
Bonferroni-adjusted significance level is used for pairwise
comparisons, and the non-parametric bootstrap is used to construct
confidence intervals.

    A no less important practical contribution of the thesis is the
empirically grounded conclusion that the quality of training data
takes precedence over its volume: a balanced mixed sample of 500
examples reduces the error rate from 18.2% for the baseline to 0.6%
— by a factor of more than 30 — with only one misclassification on a
500-query benchmark and a robust transfer to the real user-query
distribution, whereas scaling up purely synthetic data degrades quality
due to generation artefacts. This result has direct practical
applications in the development of production AI agents.

    Throughout the work A. V. Goroshko demonstrated strong
independence, systems thinking, and the ability to carry an engineering
solution through to a finished, documented result; he reacted
constructively to comments, promptly introduced the necessary changes
and meticulously prepared the final research materials.

    The intermediate results of the thesis received external
recognition: A. V. Goroshko presented his work at the Congress of Young
Scientists of ITMO University (section "Software Engineering and
Technologies") and was awarded a first-degree diploma, which provides
additional evidence of the academic and practical significance of the
research.

    The bachelor's thesis of A. V. Goroshko is a complete and
independent piece of research that meets all the requirements set for
bachelor's qualification works in the field of study 02.03.01
"Mathematics and Computer Science". As the scientific supervisor I
believe that this thesis is ready for defence and recommend that it be
graded "excellent (A)".
